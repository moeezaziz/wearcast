WearCast Native Study

Three directions to make WearCast feel like an iPhone app, not a webpage.

Each concept keeps the current product structure of Today, Wardrobe, and Settings, but changes the interaction language. The goal is stronger hierarchy, more tactile depth, and less “stack of cards on a web page” energy.

1. Weather Deck

Lean into a native iOS weather-card feel. The app opens with a single dominant weather deck, then the outfit breakdown lives below as tactile tiles. It feels fast, punchy, and familiar.

2. Live Canvas

This direction feels more premium and app-native by treating the weather as the scene and sliding content up from a bottom sheet. The top of the screen becomes atmospheric, while actions feel anchored and mobile-first.

This version makes WearCast feel like a personal daily planner. Less “weather app,” more “morning routine companion.” It gives the product a calmer, editorial app personality while still feeling native.
