# WearCast Phase 1 Positioning

This document is the canonical messaging pack for Phase 1 of the launch-readiness plan.

It exists to keep the app focused around one audience, one promise, and one primary outcome:

- open WearCast
- see what to wear today
- trust the recommendation
- come back tomorrow

## Positioning Summary

### Category

WearCast is a weather-smart outfit recommendation app powered by your real wardrobe.

### Core promise

WearCast helps you decide what to wear today with weather-smart outfit recommendations that get better as you add your own wardrobe.

### Primary outcome

Help people get dressed faster and with more confidence.

### Target launch audience

- urban iPhone users
- style-aware but not fashion-expert
- commute through changing weather
- want to look intentional without overthinking it
- likely professionals, students, and city dwellers ages 22-40

## Product Framing

### What WearCast is

- a daily outfit decision app
- a weather-aware recommendation tool
- a closet-aware assistant once the user adds wardrobe items

### What WearCast is not

- a generic weather app
- a heavy closet inventory tool
- a fashion social network
- a pure AI styling playground

### Launch framing

WearCast should feel like one product:

"What to wear today, based on the weather and your closet."

Not three products:

- weather utility
- closet organizer
- AI stylist

## Canonical Messaging

### Hero line

What to wear today, based on the weather and your closet.

### Secondary line

Get weather-smart outfit recommendations that get better as you add the clothes you already own.

### One-sentence value prop

WearCast helps you get dressed faster with live-weather outfit picks grounded in your real wardrobe.

### Short value prop

Weather-smart outfits from your closet.

### Product description

WearCast gives you a better answer to "what should I wear today?" Start with live weather, then add a few staples so the app can recommend looks from what you already own.

## Messaging Priorities

Every major surface should reinforce these ideas in this order:

1. Today
2. Weather-smart
3. Your closet
4. Faster decisions
5. Better daily outfits

If copy has to be shortened, drop lower-priority ideas first.

## Voice And Tone

### Sound like

- calm
- confident
- useful
- modern
- premium but not luxury
- style-aware without sounding intimidating

### Avoid sounding like

- a generic weather utility
- a technical AI demo
- a fashion magazine
- a complicated wardrobe database
- an internal product experiment

### Copy principles

- lead with the user outcome, not the feature
- use "today" often
- say "closet" or "wardrobe" only when it helps explain the payoff
- prefer "outfit recommendation" or "what to wear" over abstract "styling"
- keep claims grounded and believable

## Approved Phrases

Use often:

- what to wear today
- weather-smart outfit recommendation
- from your closet
- clothes you already own
- daily outfit picks
- build better recommendations from your wardrobe
- get dressed faster

Use sparingly:

- styling
- curated
- fashion-forward
- assistant
- collection

Avoid:

- forecast-led styling
- unlock your wardrobe
- product validation
- profile tab validation
- AI fashion engine
- organize your entire closet

## In-App Messaging Rules

### Today tab

Should always answer:

- what is the weather doing
- what should I wear
- how can my closet improve this

### Wardrobe tab

Should always feel like:

- a means to better recommendations
- not an inventory chore

### Settings

Should feel:

- quieter
- secondary
- useful but not central to the product story

## Positioning Guardrails For Future Work

Before shipping a new feature, ask:

1. Does this help the user decide what to wear today?
2. Does this strengthen the weather + wardrobe loop?
3. Does this make the app feel simpler or more complicated?
4. Would this help a paying user come back tomorrow?

If the answer to the first two questions is no, it should not block launch.

## Phase 1 Deliverables

Phase 1 is considered complete when:

- top-level in-app copy consistently reflects this positioning
- App Store messaging is drafted from this document
- product decisions refer back to one audience and one promise
- new launch work uses "Today + Your Wardrobe" as the default framing
